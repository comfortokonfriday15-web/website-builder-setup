## 1. 90-Day Growth Roadmap

### Month 1 — July: Launch & Validation

**Theme:** Prove the content model works. Build initial audience. Validate which niches drive engagement.

| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Facebook followers | 500 | Baseline social proof for prospects who visit profile |
| Avg post reach | 500 | Algorithm needs 4-6 weeks to learn your audience |
| Comments per post | 5-10 | Engagement signals that grow distribution |
| Meaningful comments/week | 10+ | Comments that contain a question or pain point — not just emojis |
| DMs per week | 3-5 | First proof that content converts to conversation |
| Discovery calls/week | 1-2 | Revenue path opens |
| Clients closed | 1-2 | Pay the bills |

**Key Actions:**
- Post 5x/week every week. No gaps. Consistency > perfection.
- 60-minute engagement ritual after every post — reply to all comments
- Join 5 niche Facebook Groups (Dental, HVAC, Med Spa, Law, Home Services) — 15 min/day commenting
- Optimize profile: cover photo, bio, featured photos (6 builds), pinned post
- Track every post in a log — reach, comments, DMs generated
- End of month: review which niche drove the most DMs. Double down in Month 2.

**Hard No's for Month 1:**
- No secondary platforms. All energy on Facebook.
- No ads. No paid promotion. Validate organic first.
- No selling in comments. Move conversations to DMs.
- No skipping a day. Missing one day breaks the algorithm learning pattern.

**Validation Check at End of Month 1:**
- Which niche got the most comments? ___ (automate decision for Week 1 of Month 2)
- Which content type got the most shares? ___ (do more of that)
- Did any post generate a DM that turned into a call? ___ (yes = model works)

### The 60-Minute Engagement Ritual (Every Post Day)

Post at the scheduled time. Then immediately:

1. Set a timer for 60 minutes
2. Do NOT scroll your feed
3. Reply to EVERY comment — substantively (2+ sentences, follow-up question)
4. Check for tags and reply to taggers
5. Respond to any DMs that came in overnight
6. After 60 min: you can proceed with your day. Check back 2-3 more times.

### Sunday Batch Session (2 Hours)

This is the single highest-leverage activity of the week. Every Sunday, batch the entire upcoming week.

**The 120-Minute Batch Workflow:**

| Step | Time | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| Pick the week's niche | 5 min | Rotate through: Dental, HVAC, Med Spa, Law, Home Services, repeat |
| Build Mon asset | 25 min | Build the AI Studio site. Screenshot 6 stages. Record 20-sec Loom. |
| Build Tue asset | 20 min | Build/pull the Trigger.dev workflow. Screenshot config + result. |
| Reddit research | 15 min | Search target subreddits for the niche. Pick 3 threads. Screenshot best one. |
| Write all 5 posts | 30 min | Write Mon-Fri posts. Use hook swipe file. 5-6 min per post. |
| Schedule or stage | 5 min | Schedule in Creator Studio or stage with posting reminders. |
| **Total** | **100 min** | Buffer built in. |

**Alternative (monthly mega-batch):** One 6-hour session at the start of every month. Batch 4 weeks at once. Only variable is the niche — structure is identical.

### Weekly 15-Minute Review (Wednesday After Post)

Each Wednesday after the post is live, run this review:

1. **Open your post log** — spreadsheet or notebook
2. **Check last week's posts:** reach, comments, DMs for each
3. **Identify top performer:** Which post had the highest reach? Most comments? Most DMs?
4. **Identify bottom performer:** Which post had the lowest? Why? (bad hook? wrong niche? bad timing?)
5. **Repurpose decision:** Is the top performer worth turning into an evergreen asset?
6. **Adjust for next week:** Double the niche that performed best. Swap out the niche that floated.

**Template:**
```
Week of [Date] Performance Review

Top post: [Link/description] — [Reach] reach, [Comments] comments, [DMs] DMs
Bottom post: [Link/description] — [Reach] reach, [Comments] comments, [DMs] DMs
Niche that drove most DMs: [Niche]
One adjustment for next week: [Action]
```

## 4. Lead Conversion Funnel

### The Full Funnel: Awareness → Interest → Consideration → Conversion

#### Stage 1: Awareness — Scroll Stops

**Goal:** Make someone pause on your post long enough to read it.

**What they see in the feed:**
- First 3 lines of text (visible without "see more")
- Thumbnail of the image/video
- Your name + profile pic

**What triggers the stop:**
- A specific pain they feel right now ("Your front desk spends 3 hours a day on confirmation calls")
- A number that shocks them ("60+ hours a month on manual confirmations")
- A challenger claim that sparks curiosity ("Everyone says you need a better CRM. No you don't.")

**Track at this stage:** Impressions, reach. If reach is growing week over week, the hooks are working.

**Input:** Hook + thumbnail
**Output:** Post expansion (they click "see more" or view the image)

---

#### Stage 2: Interest — Profile Visit

**Goal:** Get them to click your name and visit your profile.

**What triggers the click:**
- The post was useful enough that they want to see more of your content
- They want to verify you're legit (social proof)
- They want to see what else you've built

**What converts the profile visit into a follow:**
| Profile Element | What It Communicates |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Cover photo | "This person builds automation for MY niche" |
| Bio | "Here's exactly what they do and who they serve" |
| Featured photos (6 builds) | "They've built real things with real results" |
| Pinned post | "Here's their best work — judge them by this" |

**Track at this stage:** Profile visits. Facebook doesn't expose this directly, but you can infer it from follower growth after a high-reach post.

**Conversion at this stage:** They follow you. Now your posts appear in their feed regularly.

**Input:** Optimized profile
**Output:** Follower + repeat exposure

---

#### Stage 3: Consideration — DM Inbound

**Goal:** They send you a message. This is the critical conversion event.

**Triggers that cause a DM:**
1. **Direct CTA in post:** "DM me and I'll send you the exact trigger config"
2. **Comment exchange:** You replied to their comment with a helpful question, and the conversation moved to DMs
3. **Profile CTA:** Bio says "DM me — let's talk about your workflow"
4. **Repeated exposure:** They've seen your content 5-10 times and finally have a specific need

**The 4 DM Scenarios:**

---

**Scenario A: Specific Niche Inquiry**

*"Can you do this for my HVAC company?"*

```
You: "Almost certainly. I've built similar workflows for HVAC before.
Two quick questions:

1. What's the biggest manual bottleneck in your business right now?
2. Are you looking for someone to build it, or just exploring?"

[They answer.]

You: "That's a perfect use case. I've done [similar build] for another HVAC company
and it saved them [metric]. Let's jump on a 15-minute call — I'll map out exactly
what I'd build for you and what it'd save. Thursday at 2 PM or Friday at 11 AM?"
```

Move to call within 3-4 messages. Don't prospect over text.

---

**Scenario B: General "What Do You Do?"**

*"Hey, what exactly do you do? Your content keeps showing up in my feed."*

```
You: "I build automation workflows for service businesses.
Dental, HVAC, med spa, law firms — any business where the front desk is
drowning in manual work.

Quick context: what industry are you in?"

[They answer.]

You: "Got it. What's the one manual task in your business right now
that frustrates you the most?"

[They answer.]

You: "That's exactly what I build systems for. Let me show you what
I'd do — 15-minute call. Tuesday or Thursday work?"
```

---

**Scenario C: Pricing Ask**

*"How much do you charge?"*

```
You: "Setup is $2,500-$5K depending on complexity, then $497-$797/mo for
ongoing — hosting, monitoring, updates.

But before we talk price — what are you trying to automate?
I'll tell you honestly if it's a $2,500 build or a $500 DIY solution."

[They answer.]

You: "That's a [simple/complex] build. I've done this exact workflow for
[a similar business] and it cost them $X setup.

Quick question: is this something you want to solve yourself,
or do you want me to build it and handle it ongoing?"
```

Don't defend pricing. State it, then immediately redirect to problem/solution.

---

**Scenario D: "I Can Do It Myself"**

*"I could probably build this myself with [tool]."*

```
You: "You probably could. Your time might be better spent on what you do best though.

What's your hourly worth as a business owner? If building this takes you
6 hours and you value your time at $150/hr, that's $900 in opportunity cost.

I charge $2,500 and it's done in a day. Your call.

But genuinely — if you want to DIY, I'm happy to point you to the
resources I used. No gatekeeping here."
```

This approach: 1) respects their autonomy, 2) frames the cost in opportunity terms, 3) offers genuine help either way. If they DIY and fail, they'll come back. If they DIY and succeed, they'll refer others.

---

**Track at this stage:**
- DMs per week
- DM response time (target: < 1 hour during business hours)
- DM-to-call conversion rate (target: 50%+)
- Common questions in DMs (this tells you what objections to address in content)

**Input:** Post content + profile optimization + DM trigger
**Output:** Discovery call booked

#### Stage 4: Conversion — Call → Proposal → Close

**Goal:** Book the call, deliver value, send proposal, close the deal.

**The Discovery Call Structure (15 minutes):**

| Minute | What Happens |
|--------|-------------|
| 0-2 | Rapport. "Tell me about your business." Listen. |
| 2-5 | Current state. "Walk me through [their biggest bottleneck] from start to finish." |
| 5-8 | The gap. "How much time does this take per week? How much is that costing you?" |
| 8-10 | The fix. "Here's exactly what I'd build. Trigger → Action → Outcome." |
| 10-12 | The offer. "Setup is $X. Ongoing is $Y/mo. Here's what's included." |
| 12-15 | Close. "Does this make sense? Want to start next week?" |

**No proposal without a call.** A proposal sent to someone you haven't talked to closes at <10%. A proposal after a call closes at 50-70%.

**The Proposal (Simple, 1 Page):**
- Problem statement (what they told you on the call)
- Solution (your workflow, trigger → action → outcome)
- Timeline (build time, go-live date)
- Investment (setup + monthly retainer)
- What's included (build, monitoring, updates, support)
- Next steps (sign, pay, start)

**Track at this stage:**
- Calls per week
- Proposals sent
- Close rate (target: 60%+)
- Average deal size
- Time from first DM to close (target: < 7 days)

**Input:** 15-min call
**Output:** Paid client

| Close → Retainer | Onboarding is rough | Create a welcome sequence. Over-deliver in week 1. |

### How to Know If Content Is Working vs. Not Working

**Signs It's Working:**
- DMs come in without you asking for them (inbound)
- People tag their business-owner friends in comments
- Someone says "I've been following your content for a while" — this is the #1 sign of trust building
- Comments contain specific pain points ("This is exactly what happens at my practice")
- Follower count grows consistently without you promoting your profile
- You get messages from people in niches you haven't posted about yet
- A comment thread has 3+ replies from different people discussing the topic

**Signs It's Not Working:**
- Lots of likes, zero comments (passive consumption, no activation)
- Comments are generic ("great post!" / emojis only)
- Hooks are getting reach but no profile visits (hook is good, content isn't)
- You're getting DMs but they're all from other builders, not buyers
- Same niche posts get the same engagement every week without growth
- Follower count is flat for 2+ weeks
- You're posting consistently but nobody is tagging friends

**The 2-Week Rule:** If a content format (e.g., video carousels) doesn't outperform your standard format within 2 weeks, drop it. If a niche doesn't generate DMs within 2 weeks of posting, swap it.

**Monthly Summary:**
```
Month | Total Posts | Avg Reach | Total Comments | Total DMs | Calls Booked | Clients Closed | Revenue | Notes
```

**No need for fancy tools.** Facebook Creator Studio gives you reach, engagement, and follower data. A spreadsheet gives you pattern recognition. That's all you need for the first 90 days.

## 6. Scalability Plan

### Phase 1 — Solo Builder (Month 1-3)

**You do everything:** Content creation, posting, engagement, DMs, calls, delivery, support.

**Capacity:** 3-5 clients maximum. At 5 clients with $797/mo retainers = $3,985/mo passive + $12,500-$25K/month in new setup fees if you close 3-5 new clients.

**Warning signs that you're at capacity:**
- DMs sit unanswered for 4+ hours
- Content quality drops because you're in delivery mode
- You skip a posting day to finish a client build
- Discovery calls get rescheduled 2+ times
- You feel like you're always behind

**When you hit this point** (likely Month 3-4), move to Phase 2.

### Phase 2 — Content Repurposing as a Service (Month 4-6)

**The Offer:** You've proven the content model works for your own brand. Now package it as a service for other founders and agencies who want the same result but don't have time.

**What You'd Do For Them:** Same system you use for yourself — content strategy, hook writing, post scheduling, engagement monitoring — but for their personal brand.

**Pricing for Repurposing Service:**
| Tier | What's Included | Price |
|------|----------------|-------|
| **Lite** | Repurpose 5 of their existing posts/week + 1 original content piece | $997/mo |
| **Standard** | Full content operation: 5 original posts/week, scheduling, engagement reply drafts | $1,997/mo |
| **Full Suite** | Standard + DM management + call booking | $2,997/mo |

**Why this works:** You're not starting from zero — you already have the systems, templates, and proven workflow. The only new variable is their voice and niche.

**When to introduce this:**
- Month 4 minimum. Earlier would distract from building your own brand.
- Only offer this if you have 3+ client success stories with measurable results.
- Your own brand is the best sales page for this service. "I grew from 0 to 2,000 followers and closed X clients in 90 days. I can do the same for you."

**How to sell it:**
- A client asks "How do you post so consistently?" — that's your opening
- Mention it in a Friday behind-the-build post: "I've had 3 people ask me how I run this content machine. I'm packaging it as a service. DM me if you want early access."
- Referral path: current clients → their agency friends or founder network

### Phase 3 — Hire a VA for Engagement (Month 5-7)

**What to delegate first:** Engagement routines. Not content creation. Not calls. Not delivery.

**VA Tasks (gradually):**

| Month | Tasks to Delegate | Training Required | Cost |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|------|
| Month 5 | Group commenting (15 min/day) + log mentions | 2 hours: give them the group list, comment templates, and "do not post" rules | $200-400/mo |
| Month 6 | DM triage (sort inbound DMs into categories, respond to tier 3/4 DMs with templates) | 3 hours: DM scripts, triage system, escalation rules | $400-600/mo |
| Month 7 | Story posting (you provide the assets, they post + reply to Story reactions) | 1 hour: Story template, reaction reply templates | $200/mo extra |

**What NEVER to delegate:**
- Writing the posts (hook and voice are yours)
- The discovery call (you are the closer)
- Client delivery (quality control)
- The 60-minute engagement ritual on your posts (first 60 min = your voice)

**VA Hiring Criteria:**

| Criterion | Must-Have | Nice-to-Have |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| English fluency | Native or near-native | — |
| Facebook literacy | Knows how groups, profiles, and Stories work | Has managed a business page |
| Writing ability | Can write a 3-sentence comment that sounds natural | Can adapt to your voice |
| Reliability | Responds within 2 hours during business hours | Available in your timezone |
| Trust | No bots, no automation tools, no comment spamming | Has a real Facebook profile with history |

**Where to find:** OnlineJobs.ph (Filipino VAs, $400-800/mo for good ones), Upwork ($15-25/hr), or referrals from other founders.

### Phase 4 — The "Samuel + Team" Brand (Month 8-12)

**Vision:** Samuel is the face and voice. A small team handles operations, delivery, and engagement. The brand feels personal — because the voice is still Samuel's.

**Team Structure at Scale:**

```
                Samuel (CEO / Face / Closer)
               /            |            \
     Content VA         Engagement VA     Delivery VA
   (prepares assets,   (groups, DMs,     (builds workflows,
    formats posts,      Story management) monitors systems,
    manages calendar)                    client support)
```

**How to keep the personal brand feel:**

| Rule | Why | How |
|------|-----|-----|
| Samuel writes every post | Voice can't be cloned | Write in batch sessions, send to VA for formatting/scheduling |
| Samuel takes every discovery call | Trust transfers in person | Block 2-3 call slots per week. VA books them. |
| VA uses Samuel's templates | Consistency in engagement | Comment templates written by Samuel. VA fills in specifics. |
| VA never pitches | Trust erosion | VA engages helpfully. If a DM looks like a lead, VA escalates to Samuel. |
| Samuel posts in person | Algorithm favors real-time | Samuel physically hits "post" and stays for the 60-min ritual |

### Scalability Decision Matrix

| If This Is True... | Do This |
|--------------------|---------|
| You have 3+ clients and can't keep up with DMs | Hire a VA for group engagement |
| You have 5+ clients and content quality is dropping | Batch 2 weeks at a time instead of 1 |
| You're getting inquiries about "how do you post so consistently" | Package the content repurposing service |
| You're fully booked and turning away leads | Raise prices by 20% |
| You have 2 VAs and they're running smoothly | Move to 5 clients + 3 repurposing clients |
| You feel like you've lost the personal brand feel | Take back all content writing. VA only handles formatting and scheduling. |
| Facebook algorithm changes and reach drops | Diversify to Instagram or LinkedIn. Your content system is platform-agnostic. |

### The Long Game (Year 2+)

At full maturity, Samuel's business looks like:

- **30+ automation clients** ($15K-$24K/mo in retainers)
- **10+ repurposing clients** ($10K-$20K/mo)
- **Team of 4-5:** 1 lead builder, 2 engagement VAs, 1 content VA, 1 client success manager
- **Samuel's role:** Content voice, top-of-funnel presence, key client relationships, high-end builds ($10K+ enterprise)
- **Brand:** Known as "the automation guy for service businesses" — not a tool, not an agency, a person

**The moat:** Samuel's personal brand is the competitive advantage that no agency can match. A prospect choosing between Agency XYZ and Samuel chooses Samuel because:
1. They've seen his content for 6+ months
2. They've watched him build things in real time
3. They've seen client results in his comments
4. They trust a person more than a company

**Protect this moat.** Never outsource the voice. Never let the content sound like an agency. Never stop posting — even at scale, even with a team, even when you're "full."

If it's repetitive, automate it. But the brand — that's yours.
