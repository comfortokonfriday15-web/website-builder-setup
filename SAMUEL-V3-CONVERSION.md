# SECTION ONE — HERO COPY

The hero has one job.
Make the right person feel like they are exactly where they need to be.
And make the wrong person self-select out before they waste anyone's time.

## THE HEADLINE FORMULA

Structure: [Specific Audience] + [Specific Outcome] + [Differentiating Detail]

Not: "Welcome to [Business Name]"
Not: "Your Trusted Partner in [Industry]"
Not: "Quality Service You Can Count On"

Those headlines are invisible. They exist on ten thousand websites.

The headline that converts names the visitor, promises a specific outcome, and includes one detail that makes it feel true rather than claimed.

**FORMULA APPLIED BY NICHE:**

Real Estate:
```
Accra homebuyers find the right property here.
Not after weeks of searching.
Usually within 14 days.
```

Dental Clinic:
```
Patients in [City] who avoid dentists
come to us first.
We've heard every worry. We fix them fast.
```

HVAC:
```
When your AC breaks in [City],
most companies make you wait three days.
We send someone the same afternoon.
```

Roofing:
```
[City] homeowners trust us with their roofs
because we show up when we say we will.
87 jobs completed. Zero unresolved complaints.
```

Physiotherapy:
```
You've been told to rest.
You've rested. It hasn't helped.
Let's actually fix it.
```

Cleaning Company:
```
Corporate offices in [City] stopped worrying
about their cleaning the day they called us.
We've been showing up on time ever since.
```

Law Firm:
```
[City] businesses come to us when something
is actually at stake.
Not for legal advice. For legal protection.
```

Restaurant:
```
[City] residents come here when they want
a meal that's actually worth talking about.
We've given them something to talk about
[X] times this year.
```

Salon / Spa:
```
[City] women who are serious about their hair
don't take chances.
They book here first.
```

Contractor / Builder:
```
[City] homeowners who've been burned by
contractors who disappear mid-job
found us eventually.
Most wish they'd found us first.
```

**THE RULE:**
Write three headline options for every niche.
Choose the one that names the fear most specifically.
Fear-adjacent headlines outperform aspiration headlines in this market every time.

## THE SUBHEADLINE

One sentence. Expands on the headline without repeating it.
Addresses either the primary anxiety or the primary desire of the visitor.

Not: "We are committed to delivering excellence."
But: "Every enquiry gets a response within [X] minutes. Every job comes with a written guarantee."

The subheadline should answer the question the headline created.
If the headline says "most wish they'd found us first," the subheadline answers: why.

## THE CTA BUTTON COPY

Never: "Submit" or "Contact Us" or "Learn More"
Always: Action verb + specific outcome

By niche:
- Real Estate: "Book a Free Property Viewing"
- Dental: "Book Your First Appointment"
- HVAC: "Request Same-Day Service"
- Roofing: "Get Your Free Roof Inspection"
- Physiotherapy: "Book Your Assessment"
- Cleaning: "Request a Quote Today"
- Law Firm: "Book a Free Consultation"
- Restaurant: "Book Your Table Now"
- Salon: "Book Your Appointment"
- Contractor: "Get a Free Quote"

The button copy tells them exactly what happens when they click it.
Specificity reduces hesitation. Hesitation kills conversion.

## THE TRUST SIGNAL BELOW THE CTA

Directly below the button. Small text. Maximum one line.

Format: ★★★★★ Rated [X] by [Y] verified customers on Google

If they have no reviews yet: "Serving [City] since [year]. [X] clients and counting."
If they have reviews: Use the exact star rating and exact review count.
Exact numbers feel real. Round numbers feel fabricated.

"Rated 4.9 by 87 customers" converts better than "Rated 5 stars by hundreds."

# SECTION TWO — THE TRUST STACK

Placed immediately after the hero. Before any service explanation.
The trust stack exists because visitors decide whether to stay or leave within 8 seconds.
They make that decision emotionally. Not rationally.
Give them four emotional reasons to stay before asking them to read anything.

## THE FOUR TRUST ELEMENTS

**Element one — The Review Wall**

Display five to seven Google reviews verbatim. Not paraphrased. Exact words.
Arrange in a grid. Three across on desktop. One column on mobile.
Each card contains: star rating, reviewer's first name and last initial, review text, date posted.

Which reviews to choose:
The most specific one. Mentions a detail only a real customer would know.
The most emotional one. Uses words like "finally" or "only ones" or "never going back."
The most outcome-focused one. Mentions a specific result, timeframe, or number.
The one that addresses the most common fear about this business type.
The most recent one. Recency signals the business is still active and consistent.

Do not choose generic five-star reviews that say "great service, very professional."
Those are common. They feel manufactured even when they are real.

**Element two — The Star Count Display**

Large. Center of the section or top of the review grid.
Format: [Rating] out of 5 stars from [Number] Google reviews

Example: 4.9 out of 5 stars from 87 Google reviews

This number needs to be large enough to see before reading anything else.
It is the single most trusted signal on a local business website.
More trusted than any headline you write. More trusted than any claim you make.
Because it comes from strangers who had no reason to lie.

**Element three — The Credibility Numbers Bar**

A row of three to four specific numbers displayed prominently.
Each number paired with a short label beneath it.

By niche:

Real Estate:
```
47          312           8            98%
Properties  Buyers        Years         Client
Sold        Helped        in Accra      Satisfaction
```

Dental:
```
2,400+      9             12           4.9★
Patients    Dentists      Years        Google
Treated                   Open         Rating
```

HVAC:
```
840+        Same-Day      10+          4.8★
Jobs Done   Response      Years        Google
            Available     Serving      Rating
```

Roofing:
```
140+        72hrs         8            Zero
Roofs       Average       Years        Unresolved
Completed   Completion    Operating    Complaints
```

The rule: every number must be real and defensible.
If the client cannot verify it, do not use it.
A number questioned and then doubted destroys all four trust elements simultaneously.

**Element four — The Featured Review Pull Quote**

The single most powerful review the business has received.
Displayed larger than the review grid. More prominent. More visual.
With the reviewer's name and a small avatar or initial circle beside it.

This is not one of the five to seven reviews in the grid.
This is the best review they have ever received, given its own moment on the page.

How to find it: search their Google reviews for the word "finally" or "only" or "best" or "never."
Those words signal a specific emotional experience that other visitors will recognize.

# SECTION THREE — THE PROBLEM SECTION

Most websites skip straight from hero to services.
This is the conversion mistake that costs the most leads.

The problem section exists to make the visitor feel understood before you offer anything.
A visitor who feels understood stays longer.
A visitor who stays longer converts more often.

## THE STRUCTURE

**Opening line:**
Name the frustration they arrived with. Not generally. Specifically.

By niche:

Real Estate visitor:
"Most property buyers in Accra spend three to four months viewing the wrong properties before finding the right one. That time costs more than the search. It costs momentum."

Dental visitor:
"Most people who haven't been to a dentist in two years haven't avoided it because they don't care about their teeth. They've avoided it because the last experience was uncomfortable and nobody made it easy to come back."

HVAC visitor:
"When an AC breaks in [City], most companies tell you they'll send someone in two to three days. Two to three days is not an option in [City] heat. You needed someone yesterday."

Roofing visitor:
"The hardest part about finding a roofing contractor in [City] isn't finding one. It's finding one who shows up when they say they will, finishes when they said they would, and doesn't leave you chasing them for a week after the job."

Physiotherapy visitor:
"You've probably already tried resting it. Maybe you've tried pain medication. Maybe another physio who gave you exercises that didn't help. You're not here because you haven't tried. You're here because what you tried didn't work."

**The empathy line:**
One sentence that acknowledges the problem is not their fault.

"That's not a personal failing. That's the gap the industry has never bothered to fix."

Or: "Most businesses in this space were never designed to make this easy for you."

**The pivot:**
The single sentence that transitions from their problem to your solution.
Not a hard sell. A natural bridge.

"That's the specific thing we built this business to fix."

# SECTION FOUR — THE SERVICES SECTION

Most service sections list services. That is not enough.

A service section that converts does three things simultaneously:
Names the service clearly. Describes what the visitor gets specifically. Connects every service to an outcome they already want.

## THE SERVICE CARD FORMULA

Each service gets a card. Each card has five elements.

**Element one — Service name**
Short. Specific. Named the way a customer would name it. Not industry jargon.
Not "Periodontal Assessment." But "Deep Gum Cleaning."
Not "Residential HVAC Installation." But "New AC Installation — Next Day Available."

**Element two — One sentence description**
What they get. Not what you do. What they get.
Not "We provide comprehensive cleaning services."
But "Your office is cleaned to a standard your clients notice before you do."

**Element three — The outcome line**
What changes for them after this service. Written in second person.
"You go home knowing the roof is right. No guessing. No callbacks. No surprises."

**Element four — The trust detail**
One specific fact about this service that no competitor would put in writing.
"Every installation comes with a 5-year workmanship guarantee. Not the manufacturer's guarantee. Ours."
"We give you the dentist's mobile number. If anything feels wrong in the 48 hours after the procedure, you call directly."

**Element five — The micro-CTA**
Not "Learn More." Specific to the service.
"Book This Service" or "Get a Quote for This" or "Check Availability."

## THE NUMBER OF SERVICES TO DISPLAY

Three to five services maximum on the homepage.
More than five overwhelms and delays the decision.
If the business has more than five services, display the three most profitable and link to a full services page.

# SECTION FIVE — THE SOCIAL PROOF SECTION

Separate from the trust stack at the top. This section goes deeper.
The trust stack creates the first impression. This section builds the case.

## THE BEFORE AND AFTER SUBSECTION

One specific scenario. Real or representative of what regularly happens.

Format: Before → After

Not: "We improved their response time significantly."
But:

**Before:** "A homeowner in East Legon filled out a contact form on a Friday evening requesting a roof inspection quote. Nobody responded until Monday morning. By Monday morning, they had already booked with a different company."

**After:** "The same homeowner, at [Business Name], receives a WhatsApp response within 4 minutes of submitting the form. Quote sent by end of day. Job booked for the following Tuesday."

This format works because it is a story. And the visitor reads it as their own story.
They have either experienced the Before scenario or they fear experiencing it.
Both reactions create desire for the After.

## THE RESULT SNAPSHOT

One specific client result displayed prominently.
With a real number. A real timeframe. A real outcome.

Format:
```
[CLIENT TYPE] in [LOCATION]
[THE SPECIFIC RESULT]
[THE TIMEFRAME]
```

Examples by niche:

Real Estate:
```
First-time buyer in Tema
Found and purchased a 3-bedroom home
in 18 days at GHS 40,000 below budget
```

Dental:
```
Patient who hadn't visited a dentist in 6 years
Full mouth restoration completed across 4 appointments
"I actually look forward to coming now"
```

HVAC:
```
Commercial office in Airport City
Full AC system installed and running
in 2 working days, zero business disruption
```

This snapshot is not a full case study. It is one paragraph that proves the promise.
One specific result beats ten general claims every time.

## THE VIDEO OR LOOM PLACEHOLDER

Even in the demo, include a placeholder for a testimonial video.
A dark rectangular area with a play button icon and the caption:
"[Client name] shares what changed after working with us — [duration]"

This signals to the business owner that the website was built with video testimonials in mind.
It also shows them what is possible once they have a video they want to embed.

# SECTION SIX — THE RESPONSE TIME PROMISE

This is the single most underleveraged conversion element in local business websites.

Most businesses can make this promise. Almost none display it publicly.
Displaying it publicly forces the business to have a system behind it.
And a business with a system behind this promise converts dramatically more leads.

## WHERE IT LIVES

Directly above the contact form or WhatsApp section.
Not in the footer. Not in the about section. Above the contact method.
Because the visitor is about to reach out. This promise is what makes them actually do it.

## THE COPY FORMULA

"Send us a message right now. Here is what happens next."

Then: three steps. Short. Specific.

By niche:

Real Estate:
```
Step 1 — You send us a WhatsApp or fill the form below.
Step 2 — You receive a response within 5 minutes during business hours.
Step 3 — We schedule a viewing at a time that works for you.
```

Dental:
```
Step 1 — Book online or send us a WhatsApp.
Step 2 — We confirm your appointment within 10 minutes.
Step 3 — You arrive. We handle everything else.
```

HVAC:
```
Step 1 — Call or WhatsApp us with the problem.
Step 2 — We tell you availability within minutes.
Step 3 — Our technician arrives at the agreed time. No waiting around.
```

The three steps reduce anxiety about what happens after contact.
Most visitors who do not convert are not unconvinced by the offer.
They are uncertain about what happens next.
This section removes that uncertainty.

## THE AFTER-HOURS MESSAGE

Placed directly below the response promise.
Small text. Honest. Human.

"Outside business hours? Send us a WhatsApp now and we'll respond first thing [next business day opening time]. No enquiry goes unanswered."

This converts after-hours visitors who would otherwise bounce because they assume nobody will see their message until it is too late.

# SECTION SEVEN — THE PROCESS SECTION

Visitors who understand exactly what happens after they contact a business convert at higher rates than those who do not.

Uncertainty is the enemy of conversion. The process section eliminates uncertainty.

## THE STRUCTURE

Four steps. No more.

**Step 1 — Contact**
What they do. How. How fast they hear back.

**Step 2 — The first touchpoint**
What the first real interaction looks like. Who they speak to. What gets decided.

**Step 3 — The delivery or service**
What the actual service experience looks like. What they can expect. What is included.

**Step 4 — The outcome**
What the situation looks like when it is over. What they walk away with. What guarantees are in place.

## WRITTEN BY NICHE

Real Estate:
```
1. You contact us via WhatsApp or the form.
   We respond within 5 minutes.

2. We ask you 5 questions to understand exactly what you need.
   No lengthy form. No waiting room. A real conversation.

3. We send you a curated list of properties that match your brief.
   Not everything on the market. The right ones for you.

4. You view. You decide. We handle the rest.
   Paperwork, negotiation, transfer. Every step done for you.
```

Dental:
```
1. You book online or send a WhatsApp.
   Confirmation arrives within 10 minutes.

2. You arrive. We take 5 minutes to understand what you want.
   No judgment. No pressure. Just listening.

3. We show you exactly what we find and what we recommend.
   You decide what to proceed with. Nothing happens without your agreement.

4. You leave with a clear plan, a healthy mouth,
   and the dentist's direct number if anything feels off.
```

HVAC:
```
1. You call or WhatsApp with the problem.
   We tell you availability within minutes. Not days.

2. Our technician arrives at the agreed time.
   They diagnose the problem in front of you and explain it plainly.

3. They fix it.
   If parts are needed, they tell you in advance. No surprise costs.

4. Job complete. You test it before they leave.
   If it is not right, they stay until it is.
```

# SECTION EIGHT — THE FAQ SECTION

The FAQ section does not answer general questions.
It handles objections before the visitor voices them.

Every question in the FAQ is a real objection disguised as a question.
The answers are not corporate. They are honest, specific, and human.

## THE EIGHT OBJECTIONS EVERY SERVICE BUSINESS FACES

These are niche-agnostic. Adapt the answers for each specific business.

**Question 1 — The price question**
"How much does it cost?"

Answer formula: Give a real range. Explain what determines where in the range they fall. Offer the easiest next step to get a specific number.

Example for roofing:
"Most residential roof repairs in [City] fall between GHS [X] and GHS [X] depending on the size of the damage and the materials required. A full replacement typically runs GHS [X] to GHS [X]. The fastest way to get a number specific to your roof is to send us three photos via WhatsApp. We'll give you an estimate within the hour."

Never: "Prices vary depending on requirements. Contact us for a quote."
That answer tells them nothing and moves them no closer to contacting you.

**Question 2 — The trust question**
"How do I know you are legitimate?"

Answer formula: Specific credentials, real registration number if available, real address, real years in business, and a direct invitation to verify.

Example:
"We have been operating in [City] since [year]. Our business is registered with [body]. You can find our Google listing with [X] reviews at [link]. If you want to speak to a previous client before booking, we can arrange that."

**Question 3 — The timing question**
"How long does it take?"

Answer formula: Specific timeframe by service type. What affects the timeline. What they can do to speed it up.

**Question 4 — The guarantee question**
"What if something goes wrong?"

Answer formula: Be specific about what the guarantee covers. Name the person responsible. Give them a direct contact method.

Example for contractor:
"Every job comes with a 12-month workmanship guarantee. If anything we installed or repaired fails within 12 months due to our workmanship, we come back and fix it at no cost. You call [name] directly on [number]. Not a helpdesk. The person who did the job."

**Question 5 — The area question**
"Do you serve my area?"

Answer formula: List specific areas served. Name neighborhoods. Be granular.
Not "We serve Accra and surrounding areas." But "We work across East Legon, Cantonments, Airport Residential, Tema, Spintex, and Adenta. If your area is not listed, send us a WhatsApp and we will tell you honestly."

**Question 6 — The first-time anxiety question**
"I have never used this type of service before. What should I expect?"

This question is critical for dental, physiotherapy, and any service that involves personal vulnerability.
Answer formula: Walk them through the first appointment step by step. Normalize any anxiety. Be specific about what they will not have to worry about.

**Question 7 — The comparison question**
"Why should I choose you over [Competitor]?"

Never attack a competitor. Instead answer by being specific about what makes this business different.
Not "we are better." But "the specific thing we do that most others in this space don't."

Example for cleaning:
"We do not send a different team every time. You get the same two-person team for every visit. They know your space. They know your preferences. You do not have to explain anything after the first clean."

**Question 8 — The urgency question**
"Can you help me today / this week?"

Answer formula: Be honest about current availability. Give a specific timeline. Offer the fastest path to booking.

# SECTION NINE — THE ANCHOR CONTENT SECTION

One section on every website that exists purely to handle the most emotionally charged objection for that specific niche.

This is not in the FAQ. This stands alone because it deserves more space.

## THE FORMAT

Large headline naming the fear directly.
Three to four paragraphs addressing it with honesty and specificity.
No sales language. No promotion. Just truth.

## BY NICHE

**Dental — The Fear of Pain Section:**

Headline: "Worried it will hurt?"

Copy: "Most people who have avoided the dentist for years are not avoiding the dentist. They are avoiding a previous experience that was uncomfortable.

Here is what is different here. We use [specific anaesthetic]. We explain every step before we do it. We stop if you raise your hand. Not eventually. Immediately.

We have treated [X] patients who told us at their first appointment that they had not been in years. Most of them booked their next appointment before they left.

The first appointment is a conversation. Nothing happens that you do not agree to."

**Roofing — The Fear of Being Overcharged Section:**

Headline: "Worried about surprise costs?"

Copy: "Most bad experiences with roofing contractors involve one of two things. The quote changes after work begins. Or work is completed and the invoice is larger than the original number.

We quote in writing. That number does not change unless you ask us to add something that was not in the original scope. If we discover something during the job that was not visible during the quote, we stop and call you before we touch it. You decide whether to proceed and at what cost.

If you want to see the quote in writing before committing to anything, send us three photos of the area and your location. We will send a written estimate within the hour."

**Contractor — The Fear of No-Shows Section:**

Headline: "Tired of contractors who disappear?"

Copy: "This is the most common complaint about the trades in [City] and we understand why.

Here is our policy. We give you a one-hour arrival window. We send a WhatsApp message when we are 20 minutes away. If anything changes, we call before the window closes, not after.

If we miss the window without calling, the first hour of labour on your job is free. No questions asked.

We have held this policy since [year]. We have paid it out [X] times. We are still here."

# SECTION TEN — THE TEAM SECTION

People buy from people. Not from businesses.

A team section with real faces and human bios converts better than any feature list.

## THE TEAM BIO FORMULA

For each team member, five elements. Maximum 60 words per person.

**Name and role:** Real. Specific. Not "Director" but "Lead Property Consultant" or "Senior Technician."

**The specific expertise statement:** What they are better at than most people in their role.
"Emmanuel has been matching Accra buyers to properties since 2014. He knows East Legon street by street."

**The human detail:** One personal fact that makes them real.
"He grew up in Tema. He has bought and sold two properties himself."

**The result they consistently produce:** Specific. Measurable if possible.
"82% of his clients close within 30 days of their first viewing."

**The direct contact invitation:** Where appropriate.
"WhatsApp Emmanuel directly: [number]"

## IF THE CLIENT HAS ONLY ONE PERSON

Give the owner their own section. Not a team section.
Larger photo. More personal bio. The story of why they started the business.
One business owner's real story converts better than three stock-photo team members.

# SECTION ELEVEN — THE URGENCY MECHANISM

Every demo should include one honest urgency element.
Not fake. Not manipulative. Grounded in a real capacity or real timing truth.

## OPTIONS BY SITUATION

**Capacity-based urgency:**
"We take on [X] new clients per month to maintain quality. [X] slots remaining for [current month]."

This works if it is true. Never use it if it is not. False urgency is spotted and destroys trust.

**Seasonal urgency:**
For HVAC: "AC season starts in [month]. Our installation schedule fills 3 to 4 weeks in advance every year."
For roofing: "Rainy season arrives in [month]. Most roof inspections should be completed before then."

**Offer-based urgency:**
"Free [inspection / consultation / assessment] for new clients who book before [date]."
Only use this if the offer is real and the date is real.

**Social proof urgency:**
"3 enquiries received in the last 24 hours in your area."
This works when it is true and when it is updated regularly. Do not use static numbers here.

## WHERE URGENCY LIVES ON THE PAGE

Above the primary CTA section. Never in the hero. Never in the footer.
The urgency element appears at the moment of highest decision pressure. That moment is just before they contact you.

# SECTION TWELVE — THE FINAL CTA SECTION

The last section before the footer. The final conversion moment.

Most websites end with a footer. That means the last thing a visitor sees before leaving is a list of links.
The last thing a visitor sees should be the clearest invitation to act.

## THE STRUCTURE

**The re-statement headline:**
Names what they came for. Reminds them why they stayed.
Short. Direct. No new information.

Example for real estate: "Find the right property. Book a viewing today."
Example for dental: "Stop putting it off. Book your appointment now."
Example for HVAC: "Same-day service available. Send us a WhatsApp."

**The guarantee re-statement:**
One line. The core guarantee. Repeated here because repetition builds trust.
"No obligation. No pressure. If we are not the right fit, we will tell you."

**The CTA button:**
Same text as the hero CTA. Same color. Same size.
Consistency here matters. The visitor has now seen this button three times.
The third time they see it, the hesitation that stopped them the first two times is usually gone.

**The alternative contact method:**
For visitors who are not ready for the primary CTA.
"Prefer to WhatsApp first? [Button: Open WhatsApp]"
Or: "Have a question before booking? Call [number] now."

This section should feel like a person standing at a door saying:
"You've seen everything. You know what we do. Whenever you're ready, we're here."
Not desperate. Not pushy. Just present.

# SECTION THIRTEEN — COPY RULES

These rules apply to every word written on every demo.
No exceptions.

**Rule one — Second person only.**
"You" not "our clients." "Your roof" not "the roof." "You'll receive" not "customers receive."
Second person makes everything feel personal. Third person creates distance.

**Rule two — Specific beats general every time.**
"47 properties sold this year" beats "many satisfied clients."
"Response within 5 minutes" beats "fast response times."
"8 years in East Legon" beats "years of experience."
If a claim cannot be made specific, do not make it.

**Rule three — Short sentences carry more weight than long ones.**
Long sentences are skimmed. Short sentences are read.
One idea per sentence. One sentence per line where the content is important.
If a sentence runs longer than 20 words, split it.

**Rule four — No corporate language anywhere.**
Banned words: leveraging, synergies, comprehensive, solutions, seamless, cutting-edge, world-class, industry-leading, committed to excellence, client-centric, bespoke.
These words exist on every competitor's website.
Use the language a customer would use when describing the problem or the outcome.

**Rule five — Every claim needs a proof point nearby.**
If you say "we respond fast" the response time in minutes must be visible within two sentences.
If you say "trusted by hundreds" the number must be visible within two sentences.
A claim without proof is marketing. A claim with proof is evidence.

**Rule six — The guarantee must be specific.**
Not "we guarantee your satisfaction."
But "if the job is not completed to the standard we agreed in the quote, we return and complete it at no additional cost."
Specific guarantees feel real. Vague guarantees feel like disclaimers.

**Rule seven — Write for one person, not an audience.**
The visitor reads alone. Write to that one person.
Not "for business owners who need reliable service."
But "if your current supplier keeps letting you down, you are not wrong for being frustrated."
